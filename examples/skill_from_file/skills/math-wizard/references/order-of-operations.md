# Order of Operations Reference

## PEMDAS / BODMAS

The order of operations is a rule that tells the correct sequence of steps for evaluating a math expression.

### PEMDAS (USA)
- **P**arentheses
- **E**xponents
- **M**ultiplication and **D**ivision (left to right)
- **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction (left to right)

### BODMAS (UK)
- **B**rackets
- **O**rders (powers/indices or roots)
- **D**ivision and **M**ultiplication (left to right)
- **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction (left to right)

## Examples

1. `2 + 3 × 4 = 2 + 12 = 14` (not 20)
2. `(2 + 3) × 4 = 5 × 4 = 20`
3. `15 × 23 + 7 = 345 + 7 = 352`

## Common Mistakes

- ❌ Calculating left to right without considering order
- ❌ Forgetting to handle parentheses first
- ❌ Mixing up multiplication/division priority

## Tips

- ✅ Always identify operations first
- ✅ Work from innermost parentheses outward
- ✅ Handle multiplication/division before addition/subtraction
